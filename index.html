— 01 / PROFILE

Where heat, flow, and structures collide.

I am a final-year undergraduate in Mechanical Engineering at AUST, Bangladesh, where I work at the intersection of high-speed flow physics and computational methods. My research focuses on problems where aerodynamics, heat transfer, and structural response are inextricably coupled.

I co-founded the AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML), the first student-led computational research hub at my institution, to build a culture of simulation-based inquiry. Under faculty advisement, we mentor junior students in ANSYS Fluent workflows and mesh generation.

Alongside ANSYS, I am developing competencies in OpenFOAM and building toward ML-assisted CFD workflows using PyTorch-based physics-informed neural networks (PINNs). My long-term trajectory points toward a PhD in fluid dynamics or thermal systems.

— 02 / SIMULATION & LAB

The physics in motion.

HYPERSONIC REGIME 2025 – Present

Aerothermodynamic Analysis of a Modified Re-entry Vehicle Heat Shield

AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML)
  • Conducting hypersonic flow analysis at Mach 3, 6, and 7 on a modified Orion capsule with a parametric sinusoidal heat-shield geometry.
  • Executing a 3×4 full-factorial case matrix to characterise the effects of forward-facing concavities on drag coefficient (Cd) and mean shock standoff distance.
  • Validated a corrected Cd ≈ 1.44 at Mach 6 against baseline configurations.
Target: Aerospace Science and Technology (Q1)
TRANSONIC FSI 2025 – Present

ML-Assisted FSI Analysis of Shockwave–Boundary Layer Interaction

AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML)
  • Investigating aerodynamic flutter of the NACA 64A010 airfoil at Ma = 0.796 using two-way FSI, benchmarked against Isogai flutter boundary datasets.
  • Implementing a density-based solver with SST k–ω turbulence modelling and structural coupling via ANSYS Static Structural.
  • Planning a machine-learning surrogate for rapid flutter boundary prediction.
Target: Journal of Fluids and Structures
SYSTEMS MODELLING 2025 – Present

Dynamic Modelling of a District Cooling System (DCS)

Dept. of Mechanical and Production Engineering, AUST
