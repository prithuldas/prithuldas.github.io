About

I am a final-year undergraduate in Mechanical Engineering at AUST, Bangladesh, where I work at the intersection of high-speed flow physics and computational methods. My research focuses on problems where aerodynamics, heat transfer, and structural response are coupled — environments such as hypersonic re-entry, transonic flutter, and thermal protection systems, where the physics is both analytically rich and practically significant.

I co-founded the AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML), the first student-led computational research hub at AUST, to build a culture of simulation-based inquiry at my institution. Under the advisement of faculty supervisors, the lab mentors junior students in ANSYS Fluent workflows, mesh generation, and technical writing, and organises seminars with researchers from international institutions.

I am also conducting a component-level dynamic simulation of a District Cooling System (DCS) in OpenModelica under Prof. Dr. Dewan Hasan Ahmed, modelling chillers, distribution networks, and end-user thermal loads for energy optimisation. Alongside ANSYS Fluent, I am actively developing competency in OpenFOAM and PyTorch-based physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), building toward ML-assisted CFD workflows.

My long-term goal is to pursue a PhD in fluid dynamics, aerodynamics, or thermal systems, contributing to research that advances the simulation fidelity of extreme-environment flows.

Research

Aerothermodynamic Analysis of a Modified Re-entry Vehicle Heat Shield with Parametric Sinusoidal Geometry 2025 – Present
AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML)
  • Conducting hypersonic flow analysis at Mach 3, 6, and 7 on a modified Orion capsule with a parametric sinusoidal heat-shield geometry (amplitude A, wave count N), extending the work of Juhany et al. (2024) — representing the first parametric study of sinusoidal modulation on re-entry vehicle heat shield faces.
  • Executing a 3×4 full-factorial case matrix to characterise the effects of forward-facing sinusoidal concavities on drag coefficient (Cd) and mean shock standoff distance; post-processing via stagnation streamline line probes in ANSYS Fluent.
  • Validated a corrected Cd ≈ 1.44 at Mach 6 (vs. baseline ≈ 1.30) after diagnosing and resolving a reference-area error.
Collaborators: Ananya Jahan Chowdhury, Nowroze Farhan Ifraj (Virginia Tech), Mahir Labib Chowdhury  ·  Target: Aerospace Science and Technology or Results in Engineering (Q1)
ML-Assisted FSI Analysis of Shockwave–Boundary Layer Interaction in Transonic Flow over NACA 64A010 2025 – Present
AUST Applied Modelling and Simulation Lab (AASML)
  • Investigating aerodynamic flutter of the NACA 64A010 airfoil at Ma = 0.796 using two-way FSI, benchmarked against the Isogai (1981) flutter boundary dataset.
  • Implementing a density-based solver with SST k–ω turbulence modelling (compressibility correction), Sutherland viscosity law, and Pressure Far-Field boundary conditions in ANSYS Fluent; structural coupling via ANSYS Static Structural on a C-type structured mesh domain.
  • Optimising inflation layers and leading-edge sizing for accurate shockwave–boundary layer resolution in the transonic regime.
  • Planning a machine-learning surrogate for flutter prediction as a subsequent phase.
Collaborators: Tanzim Al Islam, Sumit Saha, Shraban Sattwik (College of Aviation Technology)  ·  Target: Journal of Fluids and Structures or Computers & Fluids
